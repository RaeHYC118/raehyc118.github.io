From ion channels to intention.

My work spans the physics that generates a neural signal and the algorithms that read it back off the scalp — two ends of a problem separated by ten orders of magnitude, with a great deal of assumed-away territory in between.

I'm a research assistant at the Brain-Computer Interface Lab, CITI, Academia Sinica, where I work on non-invasive EEG decoding: SSVEP-based biometric authentication and motor imagery.

Research
SSVEP · Deep learning

How stable is a brain as an identity?

Steady-state visual evoked potentials are individually distinctive enough to authenticate a user — but "distinctive under lab conditions" is not the same as "distinctive tomorrow, with the electrodes remounted." I built an SSVEP biometric pipeline comparing deep learning models across datasets, and systematically varied the things papers usually fix by convention: trial duration, segmentation, filtering choices, electrode configuration.

What interests me is how much of the reported performance turns out to depend on those choices rather than on the model.

Motor imagery · In progress

Reproducible pipelines for motor imagery decoding

Motor imagery classification has a reproducibility problem: small subject pools, heavy preprocessing, and pipelines that are difficult to rerun. I'm building analyses that run end to end from raw data, and evaluating spectral and statistical features against learned ones under identical conditions.

Projects

Multimodal control of an assistive robot

Mentored a team of four interns who built a mobile assistive robot driven by motor imagery, SSVEP, EMG, and vision-based gesture recognition. They are the four co-first authors on the resulting BCI 2026 paper. Most of my job was deciding which parts of the problem they should be allowed to get stuck on.
